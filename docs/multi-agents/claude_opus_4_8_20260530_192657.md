# Cutting multi-agent LLM spend without cutting quality

## Executive summary

**Multi-agent LLM systems burn roughly 15× the tokens of a chat interaction and ~4× the tokens of a single-agent loop** — Anthropic's own production data on their research system — and that overhead is paid for only when task value justifies it. The cost compounds across six surfaces that single-agent systems do not have: planner re-planning, handoff context restatement, subagent context isolation that duplicates work, critic/verifier loops, speculative branches that get discarded, and reasoning/thinking tokens that bill at the output rate but are invisible to the user. A single Claude Opus 4.x extended-thinking call can spend **20,000–40,000 thinking tokens before emitting 500 visible output tokens**, meaning the hidden reasoning is 50× more expensive than the answer. Tail cost is similarly skewed: production agent traces commonly show **p95 ≈ 18× median cost**.

The good news is that none of the major savings techniques require a quality trade-off if measured correctly. Prefix-prompt caching is automatic on OpenAI, opt-in on Anthropic at a **10% read multiplier (90% discount)**, and implicit on Gemini 2.5+; combined with the Batch API (50% off everywhere) it can drive **effective input cost to 12–25% of list price** on cache-friendly workloads. Routing the easy 70–85% of queries to a small model preserves ~95% of GPT-class quality at **26% of strong-model calls** (RouteLLM, ICLR 2025). Reranking lets retrieval send ~5 chunks to synthesis instead of ~50, paying back **~37× per rerank call** on a Claude Sonnet pipeline. Replacing fuzzy LLM-as-classifier paths with embeddings + a logistic regression head, or with regex/code where deterministic, is **100–1000× cheaper** and removes a class of failures.

The risk is asymmetric: cost cuts that are not gated by measured quality, safety, and task-success metrics can degrade silently. **Do not optimise** legal, medical, financial, safety-critical, or irreversible-action workflows — EU AI Act Annex III high-risk categories, HIPAA-regulated PHI, FINRA-supervised communications, and any action with monetary or physical impact should always route to the strongest available model with a critic gate and human review queue. For everything else, the path is: instrument with OpenTelemetry GenAI semantic conventions, ship a golden eval set with regression gates, and only then turn on routing, caching, batching, reranking, budgets, and code-or-rule replacement, validating each change on the same Pareto frontier.

This report gives a complete cost taxonomy, an action taxonomy of what is commonly wasteful and how to detect it, a full trace schema, a metrics catalogue with formulas, a dashboard design with concrete alert thresholds, a 14-row optimisation matrix with expected impact and validation method, a reference architecture, an evaluation plan, and a 30/60/90-day roadmap. All pricing figures are current as of May 2026 and labelled with verification status.

## 1. Cost taxonomy: where the money actually goes

Cost in a production multi-agent system arises from **eleven** distinct surfaces. Each is independently optimisable, and the relative weight differs by workload.

### Token surfaces (the largest share for almost every workload)

**Input tokens** dominate retrieval-heavy and long-system-prompt workloads. Current per-million list prices: Claude Opus 4.5–4.8 at **$5/MTok**, Claude Sonnet 4.6 at **$3/MTok**, Claude Haiku 4.5 at **$1/MTok**; GPT-5.5 at **$5/MTok**, GPT-5.4 at **$2.50/MTok**, GPT-5.4-mini at **$0.75/MTok**, GPT-5.4-nano at **$0.20/MTok**; Gemini 3.5 Flash at **$1.50/MTok**, Gemini 3.1 Pro at **$2/MTok** ≤200K and $4/MTok >200K, Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite at **$0.10/MTok**. (Verified against the providers' May 2026 pricing pages; note Opus 4.7+ uses a new tokenizer that consumes up to 35% more tokens per character than older models.)

**Cached input tokens** are the single biggest lever on long-context agents. All three providers now charge cache reads at roughly **10% of the base input price (90% discount)**: Claude Opus reads at $0.50/MTok, Sonnet at $0.30, Haiku at $0.10; GPT-5.5 cached at $0.50, GPT-5.4 at $0.25; Gemini 2.5 Pro cached at $0.125/MTok. Cache writes cost more on Anthropic (1.25× for 5-minute TTL, 2× for 1-hour TTL) and have no extra charge on OpenAI; Gemini explicit caching adds a per-hour storage fee ($1/MTok/hr for 2.5 Flash, $4.50/MTok/hr for 2.5 Pro and 3.1 Pro). Minimum cacheable prefix is **1,024 tokens** for most current models but **4,096 tokens** for Opus 4.5/4.6 and Haiku 4.5.

**Output tokens** are 5–6× more expensive than input on every provider: Opus 4.8 output at **$25/MTok**, Sonnet 4.6 at **$15/MTok**, Haiku 4.5 at **$5/MTok**; GPT-5.5 output at **$30/MTok**, GPT-5.4 at **$15/MTok**, mini/nano at $4.50/$1.25; Gemini 3.5 Flash at **$9/MTok**, 3.1 Pro at $12/$18, 2.5 Pro at $10/$15. Output cost is the right place to bound first.

**Reasoning / thinking tokens** are the silent budget killer. All three providers converge on the same rule: **reasoning tokens bill as output tokens at the standard output rate**, whether OpenAI's `completion_tokens_details.reasoning_tokens`, Anthropic's extended-thinking, or Gemini's `thoughtsTokenCount`. A single complex GPT-5.5 query can spend 10,000+ hidden thinking tokens at $30/MTok = $0.30 versus a $0.006 visible answer. Setting `max_completion_tokens` too low silently produces empty responses because the budget is consumed before a single visible token is emitted.

**Retrieval / embedding tokens.** Text-embedding-3-small at **$0.02/MTok**, text-embedding-3-large at $0.13, Gemini embeddings at $0.15–0.20, Cohere embed at similar rates. For a 10M-document corpus at 500 tokens/chunk this is a one-time $100–$650 indexing spend; the recurring cost is the per-query embedding (negligible) and the per-query input tokens for retrieved chunks (potentially huge — top-50 × 500 tokens = 25,000 input tokens per query).

### Tool, retrieval, and execution surfaces

**Vector store costs** (Pinecone, Weaviate, Turbopuffer, pgvector) are dominated by storage GB-month and query QPS. Typical Pinecone serverless: $0.33/GB-month plus $0.04/M write units, $0.04/M read units — material at scale but rarely the bottleneck.

**Server-side tool calls** are now meaningful line items. Anthropic web search **$10 per 1,000 searches** plus retrieved content tokens; OpenAI web search on reasoning models $10/1k calls (full content billed at model rate) or $25/1k on non-reasoning models (content free); OpenAI file search **$2.50/1k calls** plus $0.10/GB-day storage; Anthropic code execution $0.05/container-hour with 1,550 free hours/month; OpenAI container runtime $0.03–$1.92 per 20-minute session depending on size; Gemini grounded search **$14/1k prompts** (Gemini 3 family) or $35/1k (2.5 family), free up to 1,500–5,000 RPD.

**Tool-use system overhead** is non-trivial: enabling `tools` on Claude Opus 4.8 adds 290–410 input tokens before any tool is called; each tool's name + description + schema adds further input tokens; `tool_use`/`tool_result` blocks bill as standard tokens.

### Storage, retries, loops, and orchestration

**Storage**: trace storage in your observability platform (Langfuse free up to 50K observations/month, LangSmith $0.50–$5/1k traces over base, Datadog $160/mo for 100K LLM spans).

**Retries** add a multiplicative cost dependent on retry policy. If 8% of calls retry once and 1% retry twice, the retry tax is **9% on top of base cost**, plus any cascading retry of downstream tools.

**Critic / verifier loops**: each refinement pass is approximately a full generation + critique = **3× base cost per cycle** (Self-Refine, Reflexion). For tasks where critique does not help — Brinkmann et al. (Jan 2025) showed *zero* gain on product attribute extraction — this is pure waste.

**Planner loops**: Anthropic explicitly reports their early multi-agent prototype would "spawn 50 subagents for simple queries" before they capped subagent fan-out by query complexity.

**Handoff overhead**: Cognition's published thesis is that each agent re-reads conversation history on handoff, and LangChain's τ-bench benchmark showed a supervisor architecture spending consistently more tokens than a swarm because of "translation" overhead between agents.

**Orchestration overhead**: framework abstraction layers, retries inside the framework, and double-instrumentation can add 5–15% on top of provider charges, observable as the gap between provider-billed tokens and platform-attributed tokens.

### How multi-agent compounds versus single-agent

Anthropic's verbatim finding: *"agents typically use about 4× more tokens than chat interactions, and multi-agent systems use about 15× more tokens than chats."* That is **~15× chat → ~3.75× single-agent** when the denominators are aligned. A separate UIUC study reports **4–220× more tokens** for multi-agent vs single-agent across 7 datasets and 6 models (the 220× upper bound is for naive frameworks; Anthropic's 4× is their tuned production system). Anthropic also reports that in their internal research eval, **token usage alone explained 80% of performance variance** — meaning multi-agent gains are mostly paid for by spending more tokens, not by architectural magic. Tran & Kiela (Stanford, 2025) reinforce this: under equal token budgets, single-agent systems match or beat multi-agent on multi-hop reasoning. The implication for cost optimisation is that **the architecture decision is itself a cost decision**, and the right default is "the simplest configuration that hits your quality bar."

## 2. Unnecessary-action taxonomy

Twelve action classes are systematically wasteful in production multi-agent systems. Each is independently detectable and removable.

**Duplicate tool calls** occur when two subagents or two steps invoke the same tool with semantically identical arguments. Anthropic explicitly documents this: in their early system, "one subagent explored the 2021 automotive chip crisis while two others duplicated work investigating current 2025 supply chains."

**Unused retrieval** is the dominant retrieval waste mode. Pulling top-50 chunks and only quoting 2 in the final answer means 96% of the input tokens spent on retrieved context were unnecessary. Reranking then top-k pruning solves this directly.

**Overlong context (whole-document stuffing)** is the related token waste. The LangChain τ-bench distractor study showed single-agent token use scaling sharply with distractor domains, and TokenCrush case studies report compression from 263K characters to 2K (~99%) with preserved meaning. "Lost in the middle" (Liu et al. 2023) means this is **not just costly but actively harmful** — pruning improves both cost and quality simultaneously.

**Repeated planning** happens when the planner re-decomposes the same task after each subagent return rather than committing to a plan and only re-planning on a real signal. Bounded by fixed plan templates plus a re-plan trigger gate.

**Excessive self-reflection** is the Reflexion/Self-Refine failure mode: each loop costs ~3× base, marginal quality gain decays after the second iteration, and on some task families (product attribute extraction, extractive QA) there is no gain at all. Best-of-N revisions yield "only a marginal improvement" despite linear cost growth (arXiv 2510.24469).

**Unnecessary critic calls** happen when a critic runs unconditionally instead of being gated by uncertainty, risk, or cost. A cheap heuristic — judge confidence floor, output-schema validity, retrieval coverage — should precede the critic invocation.

**Low-value agent handoffs** are handoffs where a worker agent's output could have been produced by the originating agent without context restatement overhead. Each handoff costs the restated context tokens plus the next agent's system prompt tokens.

**Failed parse retries** are common when JSON or tool-call arguments fail validation and the agent retries with the same prompt. Structured outputs (OpenAI strict mode, Outlines/Instructor) eliminate this class of retry entirely.

**Speculative branches that are not used** appear in parallel-fanout patterns where N branches run but only one is selected; the unused branches are sunk cost. Cap branch count by uncertainty signal rather than always fanning out N.

**Long hidden reasoning** — the GPT-5/o-series and Anthropic extended-thinking failure mode discussed above. Setting `reasoning_effort` or `thinking.budget_tokens` per task class (low/medium/high) and monitoring `reasoning_tokens / output_tokens` ratio per route exposes the bloat.

**Verbose structured outputs** — long field names, deeply nested schemas, verbose enum values, redundant fields. Short keys, flat schemas, and dropping optional fields typically saves 5–15% of output tokens.

**Deterministic tasks sent to an LLM** — date parsing, regex extraction, arithmetic, currency conversion, keyword classification, schema validation. Replacing these with code or rules is 100–1000× cheaper and removes an entire failure mode.

## 3. Detecting unnecessary actions

Detection is layered. No single signal catches all twelve waste modes.

**Deterministic rules** catch the obvious cases offline by replaying traces: duplicate `tool_arguments_hash` within a thread, retrieval-chunk `id` appearing in retrieved set but not in any subsequent answer or tool call, more than N planner spans per task, more than M critic loops, agent hops exceeding a budget.

**Trace analysis** with the schema in §4 supports queries like "fraction of tool calls whose result is referenced in any downstream span by content overlap or embedding similarity ≥ 0.8" and "fraction of retrieval chunks cited in the final answer by string match or by LLM-judge."

**Counterfactual replay** runs the same task with an action removed (e.g., skip the critic, skip the planner, route to weaker model) and compares outcome on the golden set. The action is necessary only if removal degrades outcome by a statistically significant margin (McNemar's test for paired binary outcomes, Wilcoxon for continuous scores).

**Ablation testing** is the structured generalisation: hold all other dimensions constant and toggle one component at a time on a stratified 200–500 task sample. This is the same protocol Anthropic uses for their internal research evals.

**Evaluator-based labelling** uses a calibrated LLM-judge on a sample of production traces to label each tool call as "necessary / nice-to-have / unnecessary," each critic loop as "improved / no-change / degraded," and each retrieval chunk as "cited / supportive / unused." The judge is itself calibrated against ~50–100 expert-labelled traces with target Cohen's κ ≥ 0.7 or Krippendorff's α ≥ 0.80.

**Human review sampling** is the calibration ground truth. Sample stratified by user segment × task type × failure mode, oversample errors (~5–10% healthy + 100% errors is the standard ratio).

**Final-answer evidence linkage** is the cheapest retrieval-usefulness signal: parse the final answer for citations or quoted spans and check which retrieved chunks appear. RAGAS' Context Precision and Context Recall formulas are the canonical operationalisations.

**Tool-result usage tracking** instruments each tool's return value with a content hash and checks whether that content appears in any downstream LLM input. If not, the call is dead-on-arrival.

**Task-outcome correlation** uses a logistic regression of task success on per-step features (was-planner-called, critic-iterations, tool-call-count, retrieved-chunks). Coefficients near zero indicate the feature has no effect on success — i.e., the action is unnecessary.

## 4. Tracking schema

A single, denormalised wide row per agent step. Every field is mapped to **OpenTelemetry GenAI semantic conventions** (still "Development" stability as of semconv 1.41, May 2026 — opt in via `OTEL_SEMCONV_STABILITY_OPT_IN=gen_ai_latest_experimental`). Field names below are Postgres/ClickHouse-style; the right-hand column shows the OTel attribute mapping.

```sql
CREATE TABLE agent_step_traces (
  -- Identity
  trace_id              UUID         NOT NULL,  -- OTel trace_id
  thread_id             UUID         NOT NULL,  -- gen_ai.conversation.id
  user_id_hash          CHAR(64)     NOT NULL,  -- SHA-256 of user id; PII boundary
  task_id               UUID         NOT NULL,  -- gen_ai.workflow.name + run uuid
  step_id               UUID         NOT NULL,  -- OTel span_id
  parent_step_id        UUID         NULL,      -- OTel parent_span_id

  -- Agent / role
  agent_name            TEXT         NOT NULL,  -- gen_ai.agent.name
  agent_role            TEXT         NOT NULL,  -- "planner","worker","critic","router","tool","retriever"
  step_type             TEXT         NOT NULL,  -- gen_ai.operation.name: chat | embeddings | execute_tool
                                                --   | retrieval | invoke_agent | invoke_workflow

  -- Model / provider
  model                 TEXT         NULL,      -- gen_ai.request.model
  response_model        TEXT         NULL,      -- gen_ai.response.model (may differ on routed traffic)
  provider              TEXT         NULL,      -- gen_ai.provider.name (anthropic|openai|gcp.gemini|...)

  -- Prompt versioning
  prompt_version        TEXT         NULL,      -- gen_ai.prompt.name + semver
  prompt_hash           CHAR(64)     NULL,      -- SHA-256 of fully-rendered prompt
  prompt_cache_key      TEXT         NULL,      -- OpenAI prompt_cache_key; Anthropic cache breakpoint id

  -- Tool / retrieval
  tool_name             TEXT         NULL,      -- gen_ai.tool.name
  tool_arguments_hash   CHAR(64)     NULL,      -- SHA-256 of normalised JSON args (for dedup)
  retrieval_query_hash  CHAR(64)     NULL,
  retrieval_chunks_requested INT     NULL,
  retrieval_chunks_returned  INT     NULL,
  retrieval_chunks_used      INT     NULL,      -- post-hoc: chunks cited in any downstream span

  -- Token usage (OTel gen_ai.usage.*)
  input_tokens          INT          NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,  -- gen_ai.usage.input_tokens
  cached_input_tokens   INT          NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,  -- gen_ai.usage.cache_read.input_tokens
  cache_write_tokens    INT          NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,  -- gen_ai.usage.cache_creation.input_tokens
  output_tokens         INT          NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,  -- gen_ai.usage.output_tokens
  reasoning_tokens      INT          NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,  -- gen_ai.usage.reasoning.output_tokens
  total_tokens          INT          NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,

  -- Latency
  tool_latency_ms       INT          NULL,
  llm_latency_ms        INT          NULL,      -- gen_ai.client.operation.duration * 1000
  ttft_ms               INT          NULL,      -- gen_ai.response.time_to_first_chunk * 1000

  -- Cost (computed at ingest from current price table; cents to avoid float drift)
  cost_usd_micros       BIGINT       NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,
  cost_input_micros     BIGINT       NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,
  cost_cached_micros    BIGINT       NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,
  cost_output_micros    BIGINT       NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,
  cost_reasoning_micros BIGINT       NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,
  cost_tool_micros      BIGINT       NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,

  -- Outcome / quality
  status                TEXT         NOT NULL,  -- ok | error | timeout | rate_limited | content_filter
  error_type            TEXT         NULL,      -- error.type
  finish_reason         TEXT         NULL,      -- gen_ai.response.finish_reasons[0]
  retry_count           INT          NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,
  retry_of_step_id      UUID         NULL,
  outcome               TEXT         NULL,      -- "useful","redundant","abandoned","superseded"
  necessity_label       TEXT         NULL,      -- "necessary","nice_to_have","unnecessary" (judge-set, offline)
  quality_score         REAL         NULL,      -- gen_ai.evaluation.score.value
  judge_name            TEXT         NULL,      -- gen_ai.evaluation.name

  -- Task rollup (denormalised onto every step for fast aggregation)
  final_task_success    BOOLEAN      NULL,
  task_user_resolved    BOOLEAN      NULL,
  release_version       TEXT         NOT NULL,  -- service.version
  ts                    TIMESTAMPTZ  NOT NULL,
  PRIMARY KEY (trace_id, step_id)
);

CREATE INDEX ON agent_step_traces (task_id);
CREATE INDEX ON agent_step_traces (ts);
CREATE INDEX ON agent_step_traces (release_version, ts);
CREATE INDEX ON agent_step_traces (agent_name, ts);
CREATE INDEX ON agent_step_traces (tool_arguments_hash);  -- duplicate-detection
```

Notes:
- All cost fields stored in **micros (10⁻⁶ USD)** as BIGINT to avoid float drift across billions of rows.
- `tool_arguments_hash` enables a single `GROUP BY trace_id, tool_arguments_hash HAVING COUNT(*) > 1` query to surface duplicate tool calls.
- `necessity_label`, `quality_score`, `judge_name`, `task_user_resolved` are **offline-populated** by the evaluation service against sampled traces; not present on most rows.
- `retrieval_chunks_used` is populated by a post-hoc pass that string-matches retrieved-chunk hashes against subsequent span content; default 0 until that pass runs.
- `prompt_hash` enables exact prompt-version A/B reconciliation; `prompt_cache_key` is the value sent to the provider (OpenAI accepts a `prompt_cache_key` parameter, Anthropic uses breakpoint position).
- Hash `user_id_hash` rather than store raw id, and redact PII at the OTel-Collector boundary using a Presidio/regex processor before traces leave the application VPC.
- Map directly onto OpenInference span kinds (`LLM`, `CHAIN`, `RETRIEVER`, `RERANKER`, `EMBEDDING`, `AGENT`, `TOOL`) via the `agent_role`/`step_type` pair if you ingest into Arize Phoenix.

## 5. Metrics catalogue

Every formula below is operationally measurable from the §4 schema. **(S)** = standard / widely cited; **(C)** = community convention; **(I)** = internal, needs offline labelling.

### Cost-per-outcome metrics

**Cost per successful task (S).** The single most important number.
> CPS = Σᵢ (token_cost + tool_cost + retry_cost)ᵢ  /  Σᵢ 𝟙[success_i]

Numerator over **all** attempts, denominator counts only successful tasks — this correctly penalises retries and failed branches.

**Cost per resolved user request (C).** Business-defined: resolved = ticket closed without escalation, deflection achieved, etc.
> CPR = total_spend / count(resolved = true)

**Cost per quality-adjusted answer (C).** Analogous to a QALY.
> QAC = cost / (success_rate × quality_score), quality ∈ [0,1]

**Tokens per successful task (S).** Provider-agnostic; the GAIA leaderboard reports this directly.
> TPS = Σᵢ (input + output)ᵢ  /  Σᵢ 𝟙[success_i]

**Cost-of-pass (C, Efficient Agents arXiv 2508.02694).** Cost normalised by pass rate; Efficient Agents reports $0.228/pass on GAIA versus OWL's $0.398.

### Agent-decomposition overhead

**Agent handoff overhead ratio (C).** Tokens spent restating context across agents divided by total tokens.
> R_handoff = (Σ tokens in agent_role='handoff' or restated-context spans) / Σ total_tokens

**Planner overhead ratio (C).**
> R_plan = Σ tokens in agent_role='planner' / Σ total_tokens

**Critic overhead ratio (C).**
> R_critic = Σ tokens in agent_role='critic' / Σ total_tokens

Healthy ranges from the Anthropic research-system pattern: planner ≤ 10%, critic 0–15%, handoff ≤ 20%. Above these, investigate.

### Retry, tool, retrieval, and cache efficiency

**Retry tax (C).**
> RetryTax = (C_with_retries − C_first_attempt_only) / C_first_attempt_only

In practice computed as Σ cost(retry_count > 0) / Σ cost(retry_count = 0).

**Tool-call hit rate (C).** Fraction of tool calls whose result is referenced downstream.
> ToolHit = |{calls whose result hash appears in any subsequent span input or in the final answer}| / |all calls|

A useful baseline: ≥ 80% on a healthy agent; < 50% indicates the planner is firing tools speculatively.

**Retrieval usefulness rate (C, ≈ RAGAS Context Precision).**
> RetUse = |{retrieved chunks cited in answer}| / |{chunks retrieved}|

Drop in usefulness without a corresponding quality drop is the signal to **reduce top-k**.

**Cache hit rate (S).** Token-weighted is more useful than request-count.
> CacheHit_tokens = cached_input_tokens / (input_tokens + cached_input_tokens)

**Effective input price after caching.**
> p_eff = h · p_cache + (1 − h) · p_input

For Claude Sonnet 4.6 at $3 base / $0.30 cached, h=0.7 → p_eff = $1.11/MTok (63% saving).

### Waste metrics

**Avoidable action cost (I).** Sum of cost(steps labelled "unnecessary") by judge or rule.

**Wasted-token ratio (I).**
> WTR = tokens_in_spans_not_contributing_to_success / total_tokens

Contribution defined transitively: a span contributes if its output appears in the final answer or in a contributing downstream span.

**Cost regression by release (S).** Measured on a frozen golden set with traffic-mix held constant.
> ΔC_release = (C_release_n − C_release_n−1) / C_release_n−1

Always paired with Δquality and Δp95-latency — never report cost alone.

### Pareto and reliability

**Latency-cost-quality Pareto frontier.** Plot configurations as points in 3-space (cost/task, quality, p95 latency). A configuration is dominated if another exists with ≥ quality, ≤ cost, ≤ latency. Use the indifference-curve slope ($/Δquality your product can absorb) to pick the operating point.

**pass^k (τ-bench).** Probability all k i.i.d. trials succeed; estimator (c successes in n trials): C(c,k)/C(n,k). This is the right reliability metric for agents that face the same task across millions of users — pass^k decays exponentially with unreliability while pass@1 stays optimistic.

## 6. Dashboard design

Five dashboards. Every panel is built from the §4 schema. Standard alert thresholds shown.

### Dashboard 1 — Cost overview
Per-day spend trend (24h, 7d, 30d); cost per successful task; projected month-end burn vs budget; spend by provider, by model, by agent_role, by tool_name; effective input price (post-cache); reasoning-token spend share. Filter dimensions: project, release_version, prompt_version, user_segment, task_type.

### Dashboard 2 — Agent decomposition
Planner/critic/handoff overhead ratios over time; spans per task (p50/p95); subagent fan-out distribution; budget-exhaustion rate (tasks that hit max_agent_hops or max_tool_calls).

### Dashboard 3 — Tool and retrieval efficiency
Tool-call hit rate by tool_name; duplicate-tool-call count per task; retrieval usefulness rate; top-k vs cited-k by retrieval index; rerank precision uplift; web-search/file-search per-call costs.

### Dashboard 4 — Cache and routing
Cache hit rate token-weighted by model; effective input price; cache TTL distribution; router decisions (cheap vs strong model split); router-confidence histogram; escalation rate (cheap → strong) by task_type.

### Dashboard 5 — Quality and regression gates
Pass-rate on golden set per release; LLM-judge score distribution; human-eval queue depth; cost-per-quality-adjusted-answer trend; regression-gate status per release; Pareto plot of (cost/task, quality) by configuration.

### Alert thresholds (concrete examples)

| Alert | Condition | Severity | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Runaway loop | Any single task exceeds 50 agent hops OR > $5 cumulative cost | P1 page | Auto-kill task, capture full trace |
| Cost spike | Hourly cost > 3× rolling-7d-same-hour baseline for 15 minutes | P1 page | Rate-limit, page on-call |
| Low cache hit rate | Token-weighted cache hit rate < 30% on a model where baseline > 60%, for 1 hour | P2 ticket | Investigate prompt-prefix drift |
| High retry tax | RetryTax > 25% over 1 hour | P2 ticket | Check provider 5xx rates, tool errors |
| High critic overhead | R_critic > 25% over 1 hour | P3 ticket | Sample traces; verify critic gate |
| High unused retrieval | RetUse < 20% on a route where baseline > 60% | P3 ticket | Reduce top-k or re-add reranker |
| Duplicate tool calls | > 5% of tasks have a duplicated tool_arguments_hash | P3 ticket | Add precondition / dedup layer |
| Cost-per-success regression | CPS up > 15% week-over-week with no quality gain | P2 ticket | Block release, run ablation |
| Reasoning-token blowout | reasoning_tokens / output_tokens > 20× over baseline on a route | P2 ticket | Lower reasoning_effort / budget |
| Per-user budget breach | Single user_id_hash exceeds daily $ cap | P3 ticket | Apply per-user rate limit |

### Platform notes

**Native OTel GenAI semconv support** as of May 2026: **Langfuse** (full, OTLP endpoint, MIT-licensed self-host) and **Datadog LLM Observability** (auto-maps `gen_ai.*` attributes since v1.37). **Arize Phoenix** ingests OTLP but its conventions are OpenInference — close but distinct attribute names; bridge processors exist. **LangSmith** uses its own Run schema (no native GenAI semconv ingest). **Helicone** uses its own format; the company entered maintenance mode in March 2026 after acquisition by Mintlify, which is a factor for new deployments. **Grafana Cloud AI Observability** is the most fully-open OTel-native stack via OpenLIT auto-instrumentation, with five pre-built dashboards on `gen_ai.*` metrics.

Recommended stack for a new build: **Langfuse self-hosted (Helm chart, PostgreSQL + ClickHouse + Redis + S3) + OpenTelemetry Collector + Prometheus + Grafana**. This gives OTel-native ingest, MIT licensing, ClickHouse query speed on cost rollups, and Grafana flexibility for the alert layer.

## 7. Optimisation technique matrix

| Technique | Expected impact | Implementation difficulty | Risk | Validation method |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Prompt-prefix caching (provider-native)** | 50–90% on cached input tokens; up to 85% latency on long prompts. Effective price ≈ 12–25% list when stacked with Batch. | Low (Anthropic explicit `cache_control`, OpenAI automatic, Gemini implicit). Move static system+tools to front. | Very low — provider-managed, ZDR-eligible | Per-route token-weighted cache hit rate ≥ 60% on long-context routes; A/B vs uncached on golden set |
| **Smaller-model routing (RouteLLM-style)** | Verified: 95% of GPT-4 quality at 26% strong-model calls (~48% cheaper) on MT-Bench; 35–45% cheaper on MMLU/GSM8K | Medium — matrix-factorisation router available pretrained; threshold tuning required | Low-medium; failure modes localised to low-confidence routing | Stratified A/B on golden set; cost-quality Pareto curve; track escalation rate |

| **Contextual retrieval (Anthropic)** | 35–67% reduction in retrieval failure rate (quality); one-time indexing cost $1.02/M doc tokens with caching | Medium — preprocessing pipeline, requires prompt caching to be economic | Low | Retrieval-failure rate on golden RAG set |
| **Prompt compression (LLMLingua / LongLLMLingua)** | 4–20× compression with 1.5% loss on reasoning; +17–21% quality at 4× on RAG | Medium — local small LM for token scoring | Low at ≤5×; degrades above 10×; compressed output is unreadable for debugging | Held-out benchmark with quality regression gate |
| **Batch API for offline jobs** | Flat 50% on input + output; stacks with caching → up to 75% off OpenAI, 87.5% off Gemini | Low-medium — JSONL build + polling + reconciliation | None for genuinely offline jobs | Only deploy where SLA ≥ 1 h is acceptable |
| **Critic only on uncertainty/risk/cost gate** | Removes ~3× cost per gated cycle on most tasks | Low — wrap critic in `if uncertainty_score > τ or risk_class in HIGH` | Low if gate is calibrated; medium otherwise | Counterfactual replay: critic-off vs critic-on, quality delta on golden set |
| **Tool-call preconditions + duplicate suppression** | 10–30% on tool-heavy agents | Low — pre-flight checks (auth, cache lookup) + `tool_arguments_hash` dedup within trace | Low | Tool-call hit rate; duplicate-rate dashboard |

| **Output-token caps + concise JSON schemas** | 5–15% on output tokens | Low — `max_tokens`, short field names, flat schemas | Low if caps are above p99 honest answer length | A/B output length distribution vs quality |
| **Memory hierarchy / summarisation (Letta, Mem0, LangMem)** | 70–90% on long sessions (>10 turns); bounded vs unbounded growth | High (Letta), medium (Mem0), low-medium (LangMem) | Medium — memory tool calls cost tokens; summarisation loses specifics | Quality regression on long-session benchmark; cost per session length |

Two further levers are universal: **retry/budget caps** (recursion_limit, max_tool_calls, max_agent_hops, per-task $ ceiling) cost nothing to implement and bound worst-case at the price of premature termination if set too low; **structured outputs in strict mode** (OpenAI Structured Outputs at 100% schema compliance vs ~40% legacy json_object) eliminate failed-parse retries which empirically cost 30–50% on naive prompt-and-parse pipelines.

## 9. Evaluation plan

Evaluation runs on three cadences and three sample sources.

### Golden task set
Construct a 200–500 task set stratified by user-segment × task-type × risk-class. Target ≥30 traces per cell. Include 50–100 known-hard cases (failures harvested from production). Maintain L1 deterministic unit-test assertions (regex/code checks) per task and L2 LLM-judge rubrics for the open-ended portion. Refresh quarterly from production failures. Hamel Husain's published guidance: aim for ~70% pass rate (not 100%), because 100% means the set has stopped distinguishing configurations.

### Production trace sampling
Head-sample 5–10% of healthy traces randomly + tail-sample 100% of error/abandoned/escalated traces. Stratify oversampling by user-segment × task-type. Redact PII at the OTel-Collector boundary using a layered Presidio + regex + (selective) LLM redaction processor — never store raw and redact downstream.

### A/B tests
Run every routing/caching/critic/budget change as a stratified A/B with **McNemar's test** for paired binary outcomes (success/fail on the same eval set, two systems). For continuous quality scores use Wilcoxon signed-rank or bootstrap CI; CLT-based error bars are unreliable below a few hundred datapoints (arXiv 2503.01747). Power-analysis: detecting a 5% absolute change in pass rate at α=0.05, β=0.20 requires ~250 paired tasks; for 1% changes you need ~5,000.

### LLM-as-judge
Use **binary pass/fail** rubrics, not 1–5 Likert scales — 1–5 scales drift across annotators (Hamel, Eugene Yan). Calibrate the judge against 50–100 expert-labelled examples; target Cohen's κ ≥ 0.7 or Krippendorff's α ≥ 0.80. Run cheap judges (gpt-5.4-mini, Haiku 4.5, Gemini Flash) for binary classifications and schema/format checks; reserve strong judges (Opus, GPT-5.5, Gemini 3.1 Pro) for nuanced multi-criteria rubrics and for labelling new calibration sets. Always score the judge with **swapped position** on pairwise comparisons — most judges have measurable position bias (Zheng et al. NeurIPS 2023).

### Human review queue
Required for: high-risk-class tasks, low-judge-confidence outputs (score in 0.4–0.6 band), all complaints/escalations, weekly random sample of healthy traffic. Reviewers use the same binary rubric as the judge so disagreements feed judge re-calibration.

### Regression gates (per release)
On the frozen golden set, block release if any of: pass rate − Δ > 1% (P95 CI), CPS regression > 10%, p95 latency regression > 20%, RetryTax up > 5 points, critic-overhead up > 5 points. Track on Dashboard 5.

### Cost-quality Pareto analysis
For each release, plot every candidate configuration on (cost/task, quality, p95 latency) and choose only from the Pareto frontier. Dominated configurations are never deployed.

### When NOT to optimise — explicit rules

Cost optimisation is **prohibited** on the following pathways, regardless of measured savings:

1. **Legal advice and document review** — always strong model + critic + human review; structured outputs to bound hallucination; full audit trail with prompt_hash and response retention.
2. **Medical advice, triage, dosing, diagnosis** — HIPAA boundary enforced; PHI never leaves regulated VPC; immutable audit log; strong model + medical-domain judge; never deflect to cheaper model.
3. **Financial advice, trading, transactions** — FINRA Notice 24-09 + 2026 Annual Report apply; AI-assisted customer-facing content is firm communication requiring pre-use approval and archiving; explicit human checkpoint before any irreversible action.
4. **Safety-critical and physical-world actions** — robotics, vehicle, industrial control: strong model + critic + hardware interlocks.
5. **Irreversible actions** — sending a message, making a payment, deleting data, posting publicly: critic must succeed AND budget manager must approve AND a confirmation gate must be passed.
6. **EU AI Act Annex III high-risk categories** (in force August 2, 2026) — biometrics, critical infrastructure, education, employment, essential services, law enforcement, migration, justice. Penalties up to €35M or 7% global turnover for prohibited use; €15M or 3% for high-risk non-compliance.
7. **Anthropic ASL-3 contexts** (CBRN-uplift adjacent) — Opus 4-class models with full safeguards; do not route down.

Operational pattern: the request classifier emits a `risk_class ∈ {LOW, MEDIUM, HIGH}` tag; the router has a hard rule that `risk_class == HIGH` forces the strongest available model, mandatory critic, and human-review-queue insertion, with no possibility of override outside of governance review. Domain-specific keyword lists, regulated-data detection, and explicit user opt-in flags all promote to HIGH.

## 10. 30/60/90-day roadmap

The roadmap is **measurement-first**: nothing is optimised before it is measured.

### Days 0–30 — Instrument and baseline

**Engineering**
- Stand up OpenTelemetry Collector with the GenAI Auto-Instrumentation processor (OpenLIT recommended) in the application VPC. Add the Presidio + regex PII processor before any external exporter.
- Deploy Langfuse self-hosted (Helm chart on Kubernetes; PostgreSQL + ClickHouse + Redis + S3 backing) as the trace store and dashboard layer, or use Langfuse Cloud free tier (50K observations/month) for the spike.
- Implement the §4 trace schema in the application. Emit OTel GenAI spans for every LLM call, tool call, retrieval, embedding, agent invocation, and workflow.
- Implement the cost calculator: ingest the latest provider price table (Anthropic, OpenAI, Gemini) as a versioned JSON config; join span × price at ingest; store as cost_usd_micros.
- Ship the daily provider-bill reconciliation job and alert on > 5% drift.

**Data and evaluation**
- Build the golden task set: 200 stratified tasks with L1 unit-test assertions; harvest 50 known-hard cases from production logs.
- Calibrate one LLM-judge against 50 expert-labelled examples; target κ ≥ 0.7.

**Dashboards**
- Stand up Dashboard 1 (Cost overview), Dashboard 2 (Agent decomposition), Dashboard 5 (Quality and regression gates) at minimum. Wire all P1/P2 alerts.

**Rollout**
- All production traffic logged; no behavioural changes shipped yet. The goal of month 1 is a defensible cost-per-successful-task baseline by task_type × release_version.

**Exit criteria**: Trace coverage ≥ 99% of LLM calls; bill reconciliation drift ≤ 5%; golden set stable; CPS, TPS, R_handoff, R_critic, RetryTax, CacheHit, RetUse computable on demand.

### Days 31–60 — Highest-ROI interventions

Order by ROI per engineering-week, validated on the golden set with McNemar before each rollout. All A/Bs at 10% → 50% → 100% with auto-rollback on regression gates.

1. **Enable provider prompt caching everywhere** (Anthropic explicit `cache_control` breakpoints, OpenAI `prompt_cache_key`, Gemini implicit + explicit on long-context routes). Move static system + tools + retrieved-doc preamble to the cacheable prefix. Target token-weighted cache hit rate ≥ 60% on long-context routes. **Expected impact**: 30–50% on input cost.
2. **Enable Batch API for all offline jobs**: embeddings backfill, content moderation, nightly enrichment, evaluation runs. **Expected impact**: 50% on those workloads.
3. **Add reranker + top-k reduction** to RAG routes. Use Cohere Rerank 4 or self-hosted bge-reranker-v2 + reduce final top-k from ~20 to ~5. **Expected impact**: ~75% on synthesis input tokens; quality usually improves.
4. **Ship structured outputs in strict mode** (OpenAI Structured Outputs, Anthropic tool input_schema). Eliminates failed-parse retry class. **Expected impact**: 30–50% on previously naive prompt-and-parse paths.
5. **Add max budgets to the orchestration layer**: max_agent_hops (start at 25), max_tool_calls (start at 15), per-task $ cap (start at $2 for LOW risk_class, $20 for HIGH), recursion_limit (LangGraph). **Expected impact**: bounds tail risk; tail-cost p95 typically halves.
6. **Implement tool-result cache and duplicate-call suppression** using `tool_arguments_hash` in the tool registry. **Expected impact**: 10–30% on tool-heavy agents.

**Exit criteria**: Aggregate CPS down 30–50% on optimised routes with no quality regression on golden set; cache hit rate ≥ 60% on long-context routes; RetryTax < 10%; tail p95 cost bounded.

### Days 61–90 — Architectural changes and routing

7. **Deploy the request classifier and risk-class router**. Classifier is a fine-tuned embeddings + logistic-regression head; risk-class rules are policy config; HIGH overrides force strong-model + critic + human queue.
8. **Ship model routing** (RouteLLM matrix-factorisation router pretrained on Chatbot Arena, or a commercial router like OpenRouter Auto / Not Diamond). Threshold tuned per route to hold 95% of strong-model quality. **Expected impact**: 30–60% on routable-route spend.
9. **Gate critic/verifier** by uncertainty + risk_class. Cheap-judge first; strong critic only on uncertain or HIGH-risk outputs. **Expected impact**: Reduce R_critic from typical 15–25% to < 10% without quality regression.
10. **Replace LLM with code/rules** on identified deterministic tasks (date parsing, regex extraction, schema validation, simple classifications). Coverage analysis driven by trace data. **Expected impact**: 100–1000× on covered tasks.
11. **Compress planner/handoff** by switching to artifact-passing for large outputs (subagents write to S3 + return a pointer); collapse sequential agent steps into a single structured call where dependencies are linear. **Expected impact**: 20–40% on R_handoff.
12. **Bring up reasoning-token budgeting** per route: set `reasoning_effort` / `thinking.budget_tokens` low/medium/high per task_type based on measured marginal-utility-of-thinking on the golden set. **Expected impact**: 20–50% on reasoning-token spend with no quality loss on LOW/MEDIUM routes.
13. **Stand up Dashboards 3 and 4** (Tool/retrieval efficiency; Cache and routing). Add all P3 alerts. Ship the necessity-labelling judge job that populates `necessity_label` on sampled traces.

**Exit criteria**: Aggregate CPS down 50–70% vs the day-0 baseline on LOW/MEDIUM risk_class workloads; quality on golden set within 1% of baseline; HIGH risk_class workloads strictly non-degraded; full Pareto plot maintained per release; release gates wired and enforcing.

## 11. Open questions and research gaps

**OTel GenAI semconv stability.** All `gen_ai.*` conventions are still "Development" stability as of semconv 1.41 (May 2026). The proposed agentic-systems convention (Tasks, Actions, Agents, Teams, Artifacts, Memory) on Issue #2664 is not yet adopted. Schemas built today will need migration when these stabilise.

**No canonical operationalisation of avoidable-action cost or wasted-token ratio.** These metrics rely on transitive contribution analysis or post-hoc judge labelling, both of which are imperfect. Industry would benefit from an open benchmark for redundant-action detection.

**Multi-agent versus single-agent under equal token budgets.** Anthropic argues multi-agent wins on breadth-first research; Cognition argues single-threaded wins on long-coherence tasks; Tran & Kiela (Stanford, 2025) show single-agent matches multi-agent at equal token budgets on multi-hop reasoning. The right architectural choice is workload-dependent and there is no clean rule; teams must benchmark on their own task mix.

**Semantic caching correctness on personalised data.** GPTCache and successors report 61–69% API-call reduction on repetitive workloads, but production rates on sparse-query systems are < 10%, and wrong-answer risk on personalised data is hard to bound. Adaptive-threshold approaches (VectorQ) are promising but not production-proven.

**LLM-judge calibration drift.** Judge performance shifts silently when the underlying model is updated (Voiceflow's gpt-3.5-turbo-0301 → 1106 saw a 10% performance drop). Continuous κ-tracking against a held-out human set is necessary but operationally hard.

**Reasoning-token cost predictability.** GPT-5 and Claude extended-thinking can produce p95 ≈ 18× median spend per request (GrisLabs sample). Provider-side `reasoning_effort` controls are coarse; there is no published Pareto for reasoning-budget vs quality by task family.

**Cost-attribution accuracy across vendor primitives.** When agents span Anthropic + OpenAI + Gemini in a single workflow, no single observability platform offers reliable bill reconciliation across all three. Today this requires a daily ETL from each provider's billing export.

**Hidden-tokenizer changes** (Opus 4.7+ uses up to 35% more tokens per character than older models). Cost regressions can be silent across model upgrades; teams should always re-baseline cost on the same character-length corpus when changing models.
